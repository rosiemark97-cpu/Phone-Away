How it works

Simple for staff. Seamless for pupils.

The pouch system is designed around the natural rhythms of the school day. Each pupil has their own dedicated pouch. They lock it themselves on arrival, tutors verify at registration, and students unlock phones, using the magnet, at the gate when the day ends.

1

Pupils lock their own pouch

On arrival, each pupil places their phone in their own dedicated lockable pouch and locks it themselves. Form tutors verify during registration that pouches are secured. The pouch stays in their bag throughout the day so their phone is always with them, just inaccessible.

2

Secure all day, no friction

The locked pouch cannot be opened without the unlock magnet. No complicated setup. Pupils carry the pouch in their pocket or bag throughout lessons, break, and lunch. Supply staff need no training beyond a laminated quick-reference card.

3

Students unlock at the gate

At the end of the school day, students place their pouch on the magnet at each exit point to unlock it themselves as they leave. The number of magnets can be tailored to your exits and what flow works best for your school.

💡
How many magnets does a school need? The number depends on how many exits your school uses and how you want to manage the flow at the end of the day. More magnets at more exit points means a faster, smoother process. We will recommend the right setup for your site when you get in touch.

Common questions

Questions schools ask before getting started.

Straight answers to the things school business managers and headteachers want to know.

We have no formal phone policy. Does that put us at risk with Ofsted?
Potentially, yes. From April 2026*, Ofsted inspectors assess your phone policy on every visit as part of the Behaviour and Attitudes judgement. Schools that have been managing phones informally but have no written policy, no parent communication and no evidence of impact are in a more vulnerable position than those that do. The PhoneAway subscription includes template documents designed to help you put all of that in place.
How does the subscription work and what are we paying for each month?
You pay a monthly fee per pupil. That covers a dedicated lockable pouch for every pupil, access to the implementation document set, ongoing support and document updates when DfE guidance changes. Unlock magnets are purchased once as a separate one-off. Replacement pouches are available at cost. There are no hidden fees and you can cancel with 30 days notice.
How many unlock magnets do we need and how does the unlock process work?
The number of magnets depends on how many exits your school uses and how you want to manage the flow of pupils at the end of the day. More magnets at more exit points makes the process faster and smoother. We will talk through your site layout when you get in touch and recommend a setup that works for your school. Magnets are a one-off purchase, not part of the monthly subscription.
How do we handle SEN and medical exceptions?
The implementation documents include a suggested exceptions procedure covering pupils who need phone access for medical reasons, such as continuous glucose monitors, epilepsy monitoring or mental health safety plans. Each exception is managed individually by the SENCO and recorded confidentially. The suggested parent letter includes a return slip so parents can flag potential exceptions before the policy starts, which is the simplest way to manage them in advance.
